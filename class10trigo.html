🔗 Pythagorean Identities

Identity 1

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
→ sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ
→ cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ

Identity 2

1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
→ sec²θ − tan²θ = 1
→ (secθ+tanθ)(secθ−tanθ) = 1

Identity 3

1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
→ cosec²θ − cot²θ = 1
→ (cosecθ+cotθ)(cosecθ−cotθ) = 1
🔄 Reciprocal & Quotient Relations

Reciprocal Identities

sin θ = 1/cosec θ
cos θ = 1/sec θ
tan θ = 1/cot θ

Quotient Identities

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
cot θ = cos θ / sin θ

Products

sin θ × cosec θ = 1
cos θ × sec θ = 1
tan θ × cot θ = 1
📐 Complementary Angle Formulas

sin ↔ cos

sin(90°−θ) = cos θ
cos(90°−θ) = sin θ

tan ↔ cot

tan(90°−θ) = cot θ
cot(90°−θ) = tan θ

sec ↔ cosec

sec(90°−θ) = cosec θ
cosec(90°−θ) = sec θ
🔍 Derivation 1 — sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Proof from Pythagoras Theorem
1
In a right triangle with sides P (opp), B (adj), H (hyp):
H² = P² + B² (Pythagoras)
2
Divide both sides by H²:
P²/H² + B²/H² = 1
3
Since sin θ = P/H and cos θ = B/H:
(sin θ)² + (cos θ)² = 1
4
Therefore: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 ✓
🔍 Derivation 2 — 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
Proof
1
Start from: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
2
Divide both sides by cos²θ:
sin²θ/cos²θ + 1 = 1/cos²θ
3
Since tan θ = sin θ/cos θ and sec θ = 1/cos θ:
tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ
4
Therefore: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ ✓
🔍 Derivation 3 — 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
Proof
1
Start from: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
2
Divide both sides by sin²θ:
1 + cos²θ/sin²θ = 1/sin²θ
3
Since cot θ = cos θ/sin θ and cosec θ = 1/sin θ:
1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
4
Therefore: 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ ✓
🔍 Derivation — Complementary Angles
Why sin(90° − θ) = cos θ
1
In a right triangle with angles θ, (90°−θ), and 90°
2
For angle θ: sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = P/H
3
For angle (90°−θ): its opposite side is B (the adjacent to θ)
sin(90°−θ) = B/H = cos θ ✓

🏔️ Heights & Distances — Key Concepts

Angle of Elevation

Angle formed when looking upward from horizontal to a point above.
tan(elevation) = Height / Distance
e.g., Looking up at a building top.

Angle of Depression

Angle formed when looking downward from horizontal to a point below.
Angle of depression = Angle of elevation
(Alternate interior angles, alternate lines)
📐 Important Formulas for Applications

Basic Height Formula

h = d × tan θ
where h = height, d = horizontal distance, θ = angle of elevation

Shadow Problems

tan θ = height of object / length of shadow
The angle of the sun's rays makes this triangle.

Two-Angle Problems

When observing from two points:
h = d(tan α × tan β)/(tan α − tan β)
α, β are elevation angles from two points distance d apart.

Bearing & Navigation

Use sin for vertical components, cos for horizontal.
Vertical component = r sin θ
Horizontal component = r cos θ
📋 Worked Problem Types

Type 1 — Find Height

From a point 100m from a tower base, the elevation angle is 30°.
→ h = 100 × tan 30° = 100 × (1/√3) = 100/√3 ≈ 57.7m

Type 2 — Find Distance

A 20m tall pole has an elevation angle of 45° from a point.
→ d = h/tan 45° = 20/1 = 20m
💡 Important Facts for Class 10 Exams
💡

The values of sin and cos always lie between −1 and 1. They can never be greater than 1 or less than −1.

💡

tan θ and cot θ can be any real number (except at undefined points). There is no restriction on their magnitude.

💡

sec θ and cosec θ are always ≥ 1 or ≤ −1. They can never be between −1 and 1.

⚠️

In a right triangle, trigonometric ratios are defined only for acute angles (0° to 90°). For other angles, you need the unit circle concept (Class 11).

🎯

The 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, and 7-24-25 are Pythagorean triples — memorise them for quick calculation.

🧠 Exam Tips & Tricks
💜 Trick 1 — Memorise sin values

sin 0°=√0/2, sin 30°=√1/2, sin 45°=√2/2, sin 60°=√3/2, sin 90°=√4/2
Just remember: √0, √1, √2, √3, √4 divided by 2!

💜 Trick 2 — cos is reverse of sin

cos 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° = same values as sin but in reverse order: 1, √3/2, 1/√2, 1/2, 0

💜 Trick 3 — Proving Identities

When asked to prove a trigonometric identity:
1. Start with the more complicated side
2. Try converting everything to sin and cos
3. Use the three Pythagorean identities
4. Never cross the equal sign!

💜 Trick 4 — Finding Missing Side

If given one trig ratio, use Pythagorean identities to find all others. E.g., if sin θ = 3/5, then cos θ = 4/5, tan θ = 3/4 (from a 3-4-5 triangle).
