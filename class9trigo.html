📐 What is Trigonometry?
Trigonometry comes from the Greek words trigonon (triangle) + metron (measure). It is the study of the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles.
💡

In Class 9, we focus only on right-angled triangles — triangles with one angle exactly equal to 90°.

📏 Parts of a Right Triangle

Hypotenuse (H)

The longest side of the right triangle. Always opposite the right angle (90°).

Perpendicular / Opposite (P)

The side opposite to the angle θ (the angle we are working with).

Base / Adjacent (B)

The side adjacent (next to) the angle θ, but not the hypotenuse.
⚠️

The Opposite and Adjacent sides change based on which angle (θ) you pick. The Hypotenuse is always the same — it's always opposite the 90° angle.

📐 Pythagoras Theorem (Foundation)
Trigonometry builds on this theorem you already know:
H² = P² + B²
In a right triangle: Hypotenuse² = Perpendicular² + Base²
💜 Remember

The Pythagorean theorem is why sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. Everything in trigonometry connects back to this simple idea.

📊 The 6 Trigonometric Ratios
For angle θ in a right triangle, we define 6 ratios using the sides:
S
SOH
sin θ = O/H
C
CAH
cos θ = A/H
T
TOA
tan θ = O/A
💜 Memory Trick — SOH CAH TOA

Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse  |  Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse  |  Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

🔄 All 6 Ratios Defined

sin θ

sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
= P / H

cos θ

cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
= B / H

tan θ

tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent
= P / B = sin θ / cos θ

cosec θ (reciprocal of sin)

cosec θ = H / P = 1/sin θ

sec θ (reciprocal of cos)

sec θ = H / B = 1/cos θ

cot θ (reciprocal of tan)

cot θ = B / P = 1/tan θ

📈 Increase / Decrease Pattern

As θ increases from 0° → 90°

sin θ → increases (0 to 1)
tan θ → increases (0 to ∞)
cosec θ → decreases (∞ to 1)

As θ increases from 0° → 90°

cos θ → decreases (1 to 0)
cot θ → decreases (∞ to 0)
sec θ → increases (1 to ∞)
🔗 Fundamental Identities
These identities hold true for all values of θ (where defined). They are derived from the Pythagorean theorem.

Pythagorean Identity 1

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
→ sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ
→ cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ

Pythagorean Identity 2

1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
→ sec²θ − tan²θ = 1
→ tan²θ = sec²θ − 1

Pythagorean Identity 3

1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
→ cosec²θ − cot²θ = 1
→ cot²θ = cosec²θ − 1

Reciprocal Identities

sin θ · cosec θ = 1
cos θ · sec θ = 1
tan θ · cot θ = 1
📐 Complementary Angle Identities
For any acute angle θ, where (90° − θ) is the complementary angle:

sin ↔ cos

sin(90°−θ) = cos θ
cos(90°−θ) = sin θ

tan ↔ cot

tan(90°−θ) = cot θ
cot(90°−θ) = tan θ

sec ↔ cosec

sec(90°−θ) = cosec θ
cosec(90°−θ) = sec θ
💜 Key Insight

The word "co" in cosine, cosecant, cotangent means "complementary". Each "co-function" equals its pair when the angle becomes its complement (90° − θ).

🔢 Heights & Distances (Basic Application)

Angle of Elevation

The angle formed when you look upward from horizontal to an object above.
tan θ = Height / Distance

Angle of Depression

The angle formed when you look downward from horizontal to an object below.
Angle of elevation = Angle of depression
